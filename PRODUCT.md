# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: HR / recruiters / hiring managers reviewing Budi for full-time roles (UI/UX Design, Front-end/Full-stack Web Development, and increasingly Flutter/Android). They skim fast — seconds per candidate on a first pass — and are scanning for credible proof of skill, relevant education, and experience, not decoration. Job to be done: quickly decide whether this candidate is worth an interview.

Secondary: potential freelance/collaboration contacts and fellow designers/developers who land here from a shared link.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site for Budi Aulyansyah Ahmad Trisna, who has a genuinely unusual four-discipline background: he trained in Multimedia at SMK and worked professionally as a TV editor/cameraman (Adobe Premiere Pro, Photoshop, camera operation) before pivoting to UI/UX Design and Front-end/Full-stack Web Development (Laravel/PHP/SQL) during his Informatics Engineering degree, and is now also expanding into Flutter/Android. The site exists to support job applications: give HR a fast, credible, memorable read on his skills, education, and experience, and prompt outreach for an interview.

Real content has landed for About, Skills, Experience/Education, and Achievements (competition results). Projects still use honest placeholder slots pending real case-study material. No backend, no working contact form, no CMS — deferred until the design and content are otherwise settled.

Success = a recruiter remembers him, sees unmistakable proof of competence across his disciplines, and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

**Three words: precision-engineered, composed, quietly confident.**

Physical-object anchor: a fine chronograph watch in a dark display case — matte-black dial, machined numerals, exactly one jewel-toned accent (the sapphire crystal/hand). Not a jewelry ad, not a fashion editorial. The luxury reads through precision and restraint, not ornament.

Explicitly rejected the "editorial-luxury" reflex (italic serif headline + small mono labels + ruled columns) as the current saturated default for "premium portfolio." This is an instrument-panel aesthetic instead: a confident precision grotesk carries the type system, monospace is used only where it's literally displaying data (dates, tags) — earned by Budi's own developer identity, not costume.

## Anti-references

- Generic Canva/template portfolio look ("downloaded a template, swapped the name")
- Gold-on-black luxury cliché — overused in "premium portfolio" templates, reads cheap when overdone
- Editorial-luxury reflex: italic serif display (Playfair/Cormorant/Fraunces-style) + ruled columns + tracked lowercase metadata — the current saturated default for "refined" AI-generated brand sites
- SaaS landing-page scaffolding: uniform card grids, gradient text, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section, numbered 01/02/03 section markers, hero-metric blocks
- Cluttered resume-dump: walls of bullet lists with no hierarchy
- Leftover placeholder/Lorem Ipsum copy shipping as real content (the previous draft's biggest tell)
- Fabricated "case study" screenshots misrepresenting work Budi didn't actually do — placeholder project slots must read as honest placeholders, not fake photos

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't just list.** Every skill claim earns a concrete project, artifact, or number next to it — not a bare tag cloud.
2. **One craft, four disciplines.** UI/UX, front-end/full-stack web, videography/photography, and Flutter/mobile are presented as one coherent professional identity — the visual-storytelling background (video/photo) is a genuine differentiator, not a footnote, since it's professionally proven (a real paid job), not a hobby credit.
3. **Restraint reads as seniority.** Every glow, motion, and accent must be earned. The near-black canvas + single sapphire accent should feel inevitable, not decorated.
4. **Scannable in 10 seconds, rewarding in 2 minutes.** HR skims first — hierarchy must land at a glance. Structure should reward whoever reads further.
5. **Bilingual-ready.** Content structured so an Indonesian/English toggle (present in the original draft) stays a light layer to add later, not a rebuild.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark theme, so body text must hit ≥4.5:1 contrast and large text/UI components ≥3:1 against the near-black background — verified in-browser, not assumed. All reveal/scroll motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion` with an instant or crossfade fallback. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states on every interactive element.
